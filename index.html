The Beach Pneumatic

In 1867, the Beach Pneumatic Transit Company secretly constructed a 95 m long subway system under Broadway to demonstrate the possibilities of underground transport. Boss Tweed ultimately shut the project down as he was funding an elevated rail system.
The Beach Pneumatic Transit System was based upon technologies similar to the pneumatic tube system which allowed businesses, banks, and post offices to quickly transport documents and materials throughout a building.
